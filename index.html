About

Civil service is a body of government officials who are employed in civil occupations that are neither political nor judicial. The term refers to employees selected and promoted on the basis of a merit and seniority system, which may include examinations. Civil servants work for central, state, and local governments. They are accountable to the political executive, the ministers, and form the permanent staff of the various governmental departments. They are hired based on professional merit rather than being appointed or elected.
